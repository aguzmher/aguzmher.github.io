As an anthropology major with a passion for forensic science, I'm driven by a fascination with the ways in which human remains can tell powerful stories about identity, history, and culture

My academic journey centers on understanding the nuances of human biology, skeletal structure, and cultural diversity, which are essential foundations in the field or forensic anthropology. I'm especially drawn to the forensic applications of anthropology, where scientific rigor and investigative skills merge to help solve cases and bring closure to families and communities. Beyond the classroom, I enjoy creative outlets that balance the analytical demands of my studies. Floral design allows me to experiment with color, form, and structure, bringing a sense of peace and creativity to my daily life. I also love attending concerts, where I get to experience the joy and connection that music brings to people from all walks of life, a reminder of the diverse cultural expressions that I study academically.

And as a dedicated Disneyland fan, I appreciate the storytelling and detail that goes into creating immersive experiences-qualities that resonate with my own commitment to understanding narratives, whether they're found in bones or theme parks. Through my studies and diverse interests, I hope to develop a career in forensic anthropology that respects and honors the complexity of human life.
